Pressure Switch

Pressure switch components: interface box; battery pack; pressure sensor; blood pressure cuff with valve

Pressure switch setup

Pressure switch being used to control a toy plant

  1. Project - Adapted blood pressure cuff with interface module programmed to detect average air pressure changes. Ability to adjust sensitivity of activation and activation intervals.
  2. Scenario/Client Description - Client “locked in” with severe restrictions in consistent voluntary or intentional movement.                                                 
  3. Challanges - Client not able to consistently activate a switch to engage with battery operated or electrical appliances in home or school environment. Not able to initiate scanning on communication device. Identification of two possible placement options flat blood pressure cuff (under/behind head to detect extension of trunk with initiation movement or held around stomach/trunk to detect “belly breathing”.
  4. Plan of Action - Commercial blood pressure cuff modified and attached to electronic interface box. Could be any air bladder but cuff allows for easy cleaning. Arduino microcontroller programmed to monitor average air pressure in the cuff and detect when the difference between current pressure reading and the average exceeds the threshold value (voluntary increased breathing amplitude). Rotary dials allow adjustment of threshold value and the duration of inactivity (to prevent false, repeated activations). The pressure switch threshold is self-adjusting according to clients’ needs and the manner that it is being used (i.e., the relative pressure is used for activation – changing location does not modify the functionality).
  5. Client Outcomes/Feedback - Feedback from caregivers and therapy team working was “the new bladder switch much more reliable” and the client “has experienced much success with it” and a request came forward for a portable battery pack to allow movement and portability at school.
  6. Reflections/Future Work - This switch has increased options for clients who present as “locked in”.
