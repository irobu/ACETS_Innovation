Switch Switcher

Switch Switcher diagram. The switch activation can be directed to output 1 or output 2 through a unique combination of inputs

Outputs of Switch Switcher. Top cables go to wheelchair, bottom caples go to communication device

Switch Switcher mounted on wheelcair. arrows pointing to cables going to the head switches

  1. Project - Electronic box based on microcontroller that switches the output based on a unique combination of activations 
  2. Scenario/Client Description - Client wants to be able to independently alternate between driving his power w/c and talking via his communication device (CD) using the same 2 switches.                                                   
  3. Challanges - Currently, the client requires someone else to unplug his switches from the power w/c and plug them into the CD. Both devices are operable using switches, but neither the electronics of the power wheelchair nor the CD allow the user to change the function of his switches. Secondary to the spasticity, the client’s controlled voluntary movements are right and left head rotation, offering 2 switch sites.
  4. Plan of Action - Review of how the switches were used to drive the w/c and how they were used to access the CD (2 switch step-scan). Design and manufacture of an interface box and cable extensions to connect the switches to both the w/c and the CD. A unique sequence of switch presses and timing (not used for communication or driving) was programmed to allow the client to change between driving and talking. Auditory and visual feedback was provided to assist with completion of switch sequence.The client demonstrated that he was able to drive his power w/c, learn how to sequence his switch presses to “switch” between driving and talking. The added visual display provided quick feedback as to which mode the switches were currently in (red- driving; green-communication).
  5. Client Outcomes/Feedback - Feedback from caregivers and therapy team working was positive and the client “has experienced much success with it”.
  6. Reflections/Future Work - This switch switcher has allowed this client to independently walk and talk. There are still some glitches to work out as he continues to use the system but it has provided hope and encouragement for other 2-switch users for communication the encouragement to consider driving a power w/c.
