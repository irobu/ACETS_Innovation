Switch Access for Cellular Phone

Pressure switch components: interface box; battery pack; pressure sensor; blood pressure cuff with valve

Pressure switch setup

Pressure switch being used to control a toy plant

  1. Project - Adapted commercially availble bluetooth sets for switch control
  2. Scenario/Client Description - Client with quadriplegia due to a spinal cord injury requires access to his existing cellular phone (LG) for safety when out in the community as well as communicating with family and scheduling transportation to/from post-secondary campus and leisure activities.                                                 
  3. Challanges - Client has one switch site and would prefer to use the voice to operate his phone (only 4 commands inherent with phone). Client will require switch access to answer and hang-up phone calls. Client’s home environment has spontaneous background noises and client has a soft, low voice. Client had purchased a Q-Ant earpiece, but he was unable to press the tiny (mm) button to turn on/off the earpiece.
  4. Plan of Action - Researched 2 commercially available switch-adapted options: Click-a-phone and Broaden Horizon’s Bluetooth Switch-adapted cellphone. Researched options for commercially available car-speaker phones that have a single button for both answer and hang-up functions. ACETS Biomedical Engineer to determine if it is possible to adapt existing Q-Ant earpiece to be switch adapted (d/t small size, if so, discuss with client and receive consent prior to modification. Click-a phone software was not compatible with existing cellphone. Trial of BT Switch-adapted cell-phone-required mods for an external headset/microphone result was inconsistent and complicated with cell's internal voice commands; Researched commercially available in-car speaker phones that had a single button for answer/hang up functions and modified to be switch-operated. Determined that the Q-Ant earpiece could be modified to be switch-operated.
  5. Client Outcomes/Feedback - Switch-adapted Jabra – requires the Jabra to be stabilized to w/c as well as external mounting for switch; no private conversation available. Switch-adapted Q-Ant – private conversation available, external switch may be too heavy for earpiece for long-term use.
  6. Reflections/Future Work - Always look at the age of the technology for items that are commercially adapted to ensure compatibility, prior to recommending clients or funding agencies purchase adapted equipment.
